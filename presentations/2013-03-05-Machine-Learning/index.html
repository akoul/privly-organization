An Open Privacy Stack for the Internet: Privly

Note: This is an informal presentation given to a small research group. The visuals are useful, but you will need to generate notes.

Problem Statement

Submitting content to third-parties comes at a cost.

Countries without strong press-freedoms regularly censor content.

User accounts are often compromised.

Content is subject to continually changing permission structures and system capabilities, which results in accidental leaks of information.

Sophisticated companies often leave billions of user photos and messages vulnerable to exploits.

Online service providers own content posted to their website.

"...worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free, sublicenseable and transferable license..." -Twitpic's Terms of Service

Goal:

Give user's more control over their data.

Threat Model

Online Service Providers, Governments, and ill-willed users are potentially adversarial.

Assumptions

  • Browser has access to a secure transport protocol in all communications.
  • The user's browser is not compromised and all servers storing content have not been compromised.

Current Solution Challenges

Specificity

Many solutions graft privacy on top of a single provider.

Apathy

Users will not sacrifice even a small amount of usability for privacy.

Bootstrapping
